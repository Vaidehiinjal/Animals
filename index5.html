The Southern Rock Agama Lizard

About the Agama Lizard

This friendly agama prefers to live in small groups or colonies. It has a thin dorsal crest that runs the length of its body and grows to be around 25 cm long. It has a trapezoidal head and a small, chubby body with a narrow tail. Males' heads turn a vivid blue colour during breeding season. Males also like to sit on top of conspicuous rocks, making them a familiar sight in South Africa's hilly terrain. Females and children are a consistent greyish-brown colour, and they are significantly more shy. Normally, these energetic, diurnal lizards hunt tiny invertebrates like ants and termites. They can sometimes adapt to living in close proximity to cities and can even live in rocky gardens. However, the increasing density of domestic cats kept as pets in suburban areas has sadly resulted in a population reduction, as these new predators tend to kill all agamas in the nearby vicinity. The fiscal shrike and different snakes are natural predators of Agama atra in the wild. Eagles and larger reptiles find Agama atra to be easy prey. When the Agamas are attacked, they flee and will only return once the danger has passed. Some Agamas are maintained as pets in homes and have a positive relationship with their owners.
