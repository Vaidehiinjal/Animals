The Cape Town Dassies

About the Dassies

The rock hyrax, often known as the dassie, is a popular and social mammal found at the Pretoria National Botanical Garden. On most sunny afternoons, they can be observed lying around or feeding behind the Herbarium Building, where there are currently more than three colonies and an unknown number of individuals. Dassies are small, with a sturdy body but no tail (up to 550 mm long and 4.5 kg in weight). Brownish-grey rough coat with lighter patches above the eyes and a patch of long, black hairs in the centre of the back. The dassie is the closest living ancestor of the African elephant, the world's largest terrestrial animal, as well as the manatee and dugong. DNA provides the most compelling evidence for these connections. Although only one Procavia species has been identified, the yellow-spotted hyrax, Heterohyrax brucei (Gray, 1868), and the southern tree hyrax, Dendrohyrax arboreus (A. Smith, 1827), are both found in South Africa. These two species, on the other hand, have significantly more limited distributions. The western tree hyrax, Dendrohyrax dorsalis (Fraser, 1853), is the only other Hyracoidea species found in Western and Central Africa.
