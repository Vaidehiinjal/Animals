City of Cape Town

About where the animals live

Between Table Mountain and Table Bay was the initial settlement of Cape Town. The ridges known as Lion's Head and Lion's Rump (later called Signal Hill) bordered it on the northwest, Table Bay on the north, Devil's Peak on the south, and marshlands and the sandy Cape Flats beyond on the east. The nearest tillable ground was on the lower eastern slopes of Devil's Peak and Table Mountain, as well as in Rondebosch, Newlands, and Wynberg, further to the southeast. A track stretched south from the settlement's citadel, via these fields, to False Bay on the Cape Peninsula's eastern coast, and on beyond Muizenberg and Kalk Bay to Simon's Bay, where the East Indiaman trading ships docked.
