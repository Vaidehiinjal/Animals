The Baboons of Cape Town

About the Boaboons

The chacma baboon (Papio ursinus), sometimes known as the Cape baboon, is a member of the Old World monkey family, like all other baboons. It is one of the largest monkeys on the planet. The chacma baboon, which is mostly found in southern Africa, exhibits a wide range of social behaviours, including a dominance hierarchy, collective foraging, female adoption of young, and friendship partnerships. These actions are part of a more complicated evolutionary ecosystem. The species is not endangered in general, but human population pressure has increased human-baboon contact. Hunting, trapping, and accidents kill or remove many baboons from the wild, thereby reducing baboon numbers and disrupting their social structure.
